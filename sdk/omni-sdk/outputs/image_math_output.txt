- Inlet flow rate: \( Q = 2.7 \, \text{m}^3/\text{hr} \)
- Water temperature: \( 20^\circ \text{C} \) → density \( \rho \approx 998 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \)
- Radius to each arm: \( R = 15 \, \text{cm} = 0.15 \, \text{m} \)

- Angle of each arm from radial direction: \( \theta = 40^\circ \)
- We are to find the steady rotation rate \( \omega \) in rev/min.

We assume the sprinkler rotates steadily, so the torque due to water exiting the nozzles balances the moment of momentum.

---

### Step 1: Convert flow rate to m³/s

\[
Q = 2.7 \, \text{m}^3/\text{hr} = \frac{2.7}{3600} = 7.5 \times 10^{-4} \, \text{m}^3/\text{s}
\]

Since there are 3 arms, the flow rate per arm is:

\[
Q_{\text{arm}} = \frac{Q}{3} = \frac{7.5 \times 10^{-4}}{3} = 2.5 \times 10^{-4} \, \text{m}^3/\text{s}
\]

---

### Step 2: Find exit velocity from each nozzle

Cross-sectional area of each nozzle:

\[
A = \frac{\pi}{4} d^2 = \frac{\pi}{4} (0.007)^2 = \frac{\pi}{4} \times 4.9 \times 10^{-5} \approx 3.848 \times 10^{-5} \, \text{m}^2
\]

Exit velocity (relative to nozzle):

\[
V_{\text{exit}} = \frac{Q_{\text{arm}}}{A} = \frac{2.5 \times 10^{-4}}{3.848 \times 10^{-5}} \approx 6.496 \, \text{m/s}
\]
